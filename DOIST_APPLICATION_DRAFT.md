### 2. Describe a time you taught yourself a new technical skill to solve a problem.

"Recently, I became interested in low-level systems and nostalgic hardware, so I taught myself the basics of C and the Game Boy Advance hardware architecture to build a tiled platformer from scratch. I had to learn how to manage **OAM (Object Attribute Memory)** and fixed-point math for gravity. 

This 'Side Craft' wasn't for a job; it was for the joy of understanding how things work under the hood. I bring this same curiosity to support. Whether it's a browser cache issue or a complex API sync error between Todoist and a third-party tool, I don't stop until I understand the 'How' and the 'Why.' I thrive in an environment where I’m expected to be a 'Jill or Jack-of-all-trades.'"

---

### 3. How do you manage your own time and priorities without a traditional manager?

"For the last four years, I have been my own manager. Working the overnight shift means that if I hit a technical blocker, there is no one to ping for help. I’ve developed a rigorous 'Default to Action' workflow:
1.  **Research**: Diving into internal documentation and legacy tickets.
2.  **Hypothesis**: Testing the fix on a local clone or staging environment.
3.  **Handoff**: Documenting the result with enough detail that my 'Day Shift' colleagues can understand my logic without a single meeting.

I am most productive when I have a clear technical objective and a large block of undisturbed time to get it done. My output is my status report."

---

### 4. Doist values "Searchable Communication." How do you ensure your support is high-context and clear?

"Working in Ad-Tech means I deal with 'Low-Context' errors every day. I’ve learned that a good support response is like a well-written Git commit: it explains the **Change**, the **Context**, and the **Consequence**. 

When I reply to a user, I don't just provide the 'How-To.' I provide a 'Screen-Recording' of the fix and a link to the relevant documentation. I write every message as if it will be the only time the user hears from us today, ensuring they have everything they need to move forward. My goal is to reduce 'Internal Ping-Pong' and maximize the user’s deep work time."
