### 1. Why are you the right fit for Buffer’s "Hospitality Mindset"?

"In four years of overnight Ad-Tech support, I’ve learned that technical support is 50% fixing the tag and 50% fixing the customer’s anxiety. To me, hospitality means **Awareness**. It’s seeing a user’s third ticket in two days and realizing they aren't 'difficult'—they are just deeply invested in their project and currently hitting a wall. 

I don’t just want to 'clear the queue.' I want to create a moment of 'Clarity' where the user feels seen. I thrive in the isolation of the overnight shift because it demands a high level of self-reflection and lack of ego. I bring that same 'Gratitude' and 'Transparency' to every chat, treating every user like they are the most important creator on the platform."

### 2. How do you handle high-volume technical troubleshooting while maintaining a personal touch?

"I use **Systems Thinking**. I spent my AdOps career building dashboards and internal documentation to automate the 'drudgery' so I could spend more time on high-value, personal human-to-human interactions. 

When a high-volume scenario hits (like a site-wide bug), I don't just copy-paste a template. I use the 'Awareness' of the current situation to validate the customer's specific frustration, provide the clear technical workaround, and then **Empower** them with a proactive tip they didn't ask for—like how to optimize their JSON feed or fix a CSS alignment issue I spotted while checking their site. I aim to be the expert they can rely on, not just a script-reader."

### 3. Buffer values "Transparency" and "Total Honesty." Describe a time you demonstrated this.

"I am currently in a major career pivot, moving from a stable enterprise role to a nomadic lifestyle. I’ve made the decision to be 100% transparent with my current employer about my international relocation, even knowing it might jeopardize my immediate income. 

I value 'Truth over Stealth.' In a support context, this means I won't 'spin' a mistake or hide a known bug behind corporate speak. If we have an outage, I tell the user exactly what happened and what we are doing to fix it. I’ve found that customers respect honesty far more than perfection. This lack of ego is how I build long-term trust."
